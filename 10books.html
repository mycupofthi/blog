Girl, Stop Apologizing: A Shame-Free Plan For Embracing and Achieving Your Goals

By Rachel Hollis

When Rachel Hollis, 36, posted an Instagram photo of herself exalting her stretch marks, little did she know it would go viral, inspiring countless women around the globe to flaunt their post-baby bodies with pride. Since that fateful post in 2015, the former event planner has launched a wildly successful career as a motivational speaker, podcast host and author (Girl, Wash Your Face was a New York Times bestseller). Did we mention she is also CCO of The Hollis Company lifestyle blog, as well as a mom of four? Hollis’ latest book, Girl, Stop Apologizing, guides women on how to succeed with confidence.

1

Why Not Me?

By Mindy Kaling

Actor, comedy writer and new mom Mindy Kaling has forged a career out of making people laugh, thanks to her brand of witty and punchy humour. The Mindy Project TV series garnered a cult-like following, while her 2011 memoir, Is Everyone Hanging Out Without Me?, was a New York Times bestseller.

In her more recent collection of personal essays, Why Not Me?, Kaling hilariously riffs on everything from wedding etiquette to why stars pretend not to like sex scenes to her love of McDonald’s breakfast sandwiches. It’s a fun read that feels like a conversation with a best friend—not surprising, given that Kaling has always had an accessible, every-woman quality to her.
