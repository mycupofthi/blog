1

Girl, Stop Apologizing: A Shame-Free Plan For Embracing and Achieving Your Goals

By Rachel Hollis

When Rachel Hollis, 36, posted an Instagram photo of herself exalting her stretch marks, little did she know it would go viral, inspiring countless women around the globe to flaunt their post-baby bodies with pride. Since that fateful post in 2015, the former event planner has launched a wildly successful career as a motivational speaker, podcast host and author (Girl, Wash Your Face was a New York Times bestseller). Did we mention she is also CCO of The Hollis Company lifestyle blog, as well as a mom of four? Hollis’ latest book, Girl, Stop Apologizing, guides women on how to succeed with confidence.

2

Why Not Me?

By Mindy Kaling

Actor, comedy writer and new mom Mindy Kaling has forged a career out of making people laugh, thanks to her brand of witty and punchy humour. The Mindy Project TV series garnered a cult-like following, while her 2011 memoir, Is Everyone Hanging Out Without Me?, was a New York Times bestseller.

In her more recent collection of personal essays, Why Not Me?, Kaling hilariously riffs on everything from wedding etiquette to why stars pretend not to like sex scenes to her love of McDonald’s breakfast sandwiches. It’s a fun read that feels like a conversation with a best friend—not surprising, given that Kaling has always had an accessible, every-woman quality to her.

3

Cravings: Hungry for More

By Chrissy Teigen

Model-turned-cookbook-author Chrissy Teigen has strong opinions about food. “Chicken breast is extremely popular for being literally the worst part of the chicken,” she recently tweeted, garnering more than 92,000 likes within 24 hours.

Her highly anticipated second cookbook, Cravings: Hungry for More—a follow-up to 2016’s Cravings: All The Food You Want to Eat—includes recipes for delectable dishes such as sesame salmon soba noodles and jalepeño parmesan-crusted grilled cheese. Teigen, who has two young children with musician John Legend, shares a quirky sense of humour and healthy, no-fuss attitude towards food. Her skillet peanut butter chocolate chip brownies are swoon-worthy.

4

Becoming

By Michelle Obama

In her bestselling memoir, Becoming, Michelle Obama invites readers into her childhood, in Chicago’s South Side, and her more recent life inside the White House as the former First Lady of the United States (and the first African American to serve in that role). She openly shares much of the in-between years, as well, including her difficulty getting pregnant, subsequent miscarriage and experience with IVF..

Her tell-it-like-it-is account of that elusive work-life balance is also relatable (“I wanted to have a work life and a home life, but with some promise that one would never fully squelch the other”). While she’s an accomplished lawyer and speaker, Obama really shines in her role as an advocate and role model for women.

5

The Moment of Lift: How Empowering Women Changes The World

By Melinda Gates

The Moment of Lift, a debut from philanthropist Melinda Gates, is a call to action for women’s empowerment. Ranked Forbes’ third most powerful woman in the world, Gates has devoted her life to improving the lives of those less fortunate, especially girls and women.

In 2000, the former general manager at Microsoft co-founded the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, the world’s largest private charitable organization; in 2015, she created Pivotal Ventures, an incubation company that closes the funding gap for female founders. In The Moment of Lift, Gates writes about life lessons learned from inspiring women, as well as alarming data on issues from child marriage to gender inequality in the workplace. She gets personal, too, revealing the road to equality in her own marriage.

6

There Are No Grown-Ups: A Midlife Coming-of-Age Story

By Pamela Druckerman

Pamela Druckerman has never shied away from bluntness. The Miami-born, Paris-based journalist writes candidly (#nofilter) about a variety of topics. Her acclaimed 2012 book, Bringing Up Bébé, touts the virtues of French vs. American parenting styles (the former sees toddlers sitting politely in restaurants).

Now in her late 40s, with a daughter and twin boys, Druckerman tackles what it’s like to transition to midlife in her new book, There Are No Grown-Ups. She boldly shares the physical and emotional changes experienced in her 40s and the effect they’ve had on everything from parenting to fashion choices. As expected, we have a lot to learn from the French attitude towards aging (hint: there is no clinging to youth).

7

Dear Mother: Poems on the Hot Mess of Motherhood

By Bunmi Laditan

There are few things quite as validating for moms than knowing they’re not alone in the chaos and mayhem that is parenthood. Bunmi Laditan, who lives in rural Québec, was one of the first to capture the rawness of motherhood while simultaneously poking fun at it through her blog and social-media empire, The Honest Toddler.

Her first collection of poetry, Dear Mother: Poems on the Hot Mess of Motherhood, is less crude than some of her previous titles, such as Toddlers Are A**holes: It’s Not Your Fault, but equally as relatable. In typical fashion, Laditan—a mom of three—shares both the joys and struggles of parenting with humour and grace.

8

Whiskey In A Teacup: What Growing Up in the South Taught Me About Life, Love and Baking Biscuits

By Reese Witherspoon

Perhaps it’s her Southern upbringing—or maybe the fact that the characters she plays are so endearing, flaws and all (hello, Elle Woods)—but Reese Witherspoon is the epitome of charming. Whiskey In A Teacup is a collection of recipes, how-to’s and personal stories from Witherspoon that provides all the more reason to love the Academy Award-winning actress and producer.

Here, she offers how to be a good hostess via life lessons from grandma, how to throw the perfect book club party (menu included), and much more. Witherspoon, a mom of two teens, is also a champion of female storytelling thanks to her popular book club and successful production company, Pacific Standard Films (Big Little Lies).

9

#IMOMSOHARD

By Kristin Hensley, Jen Smedley

In 2016, LA-based comedians Kristin Hensley and Jen Smedley launched #IMOMSOHARD on Facebook—a web series aimed at giving social-media obsessed mothers reason to laugh. The mom duo quickly amassed millions of followers, leading to a successful Moms Night Out tour and a coveted pilot at CBS. Their highly anticipated new book explores the long list of expectations placed on parents and the value of mom friends who are there to share a glass of wine and help you deal, sans judgment.
