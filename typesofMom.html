1

The Foodie Mom

Growing up, the kitchen was the heart of the home. It’s where the family gathered around the table to catch up on the day; where you baked that first batch of melt-in-your-mouth chocolate chip cookies with mom. Even now, you’ll often grab the kids and head to her place for Friday night dinner or an impromptu Sunday brunch with the clan. What better way to celebrate Mom than with a culinary experience that combines her two passions: cooking and family.

Find her favourite cookie recipe from your childhood and invite mom over for a decadent afternoon of coffee and sweets. Or gift her that coveted cookbook you’ve both been eager to try and share time together in the kitchen testing out a new recipe (bonus points for laying out the necessary ingredients ahead of time). It’s the ultimate bonding time.

2

Takes-Care-of-Everyone Mom

Remember your first kiss? First heartbreak? First real job? Mom was always there to share in the joy, the sadness, the newness of it all. She helped you set up your college dorm room and checked in daily—pre-internet, pre-smart phone—to make certain you were taking care of yourself. Not much has changed. You may have your own children now, your own home to look after and a high-powered career, but mom is still only a phone call away. She continues to give all of herself—from offering advice to showing up at your door with a fresh pot of chicken-noodle soup—to ensure everyone is happy and well.

It’s time to turn the tables this Mother’s Day and indulge her in some well-earned pampering. Book her a 90-minute, deep-tissue massage and pick her up for some post-treatment tea at your place. Or create your own DIY spa day at home with face masks and homemade body scrubs. Light some scented candles, throw on some CSNY and make a pitcher of lemon-infused water to sip while you spa.

3

Green Thumb Mom

For as long as you can remember, mom was happiest in the garden. Nothing like getting your hands dirty in the great outdoors, she used to say (you’d instinctively roll your eyes during those teenage years). As an adult, you can finally appreciate her love of all things green. Show your admiration this Mother’s Day with a unique experience focused on gardening.

Build a terrarium together (stock up on glass jars, rocks, pebbles, soil and small plants) or create personalized flowerpots that’ll add a welcome dose of colour to her home—just in time for spring. You could also gather the kids around and with mom’s guidance, have them plant some seeds to grow their own veggies, such as carrots and string beans. Mom and kids alike will share a sense of pride in what they’ve created.

4

Trendsetter Mom

When it comes to fashion and design, mom is always one step ahead of the game. She was rocking leopard print long before Lady Gaga—and she practically invented the notion of hygge (chic-but-cozy blankets were a staple in your home). She’s your go-to source when it comes to books, and she has yet to steer you wrong. This is a woman who appreciates beauty in all forms, from elegant, coffee-table books to luxurious sleepwear.

Now’s the perfect time to spend an afternoon playing Marie Kondo with mom. Head to her house to give her closet a spring cleaning; have her try on her favourite pieces and curate the best looks, while deciding what to keep and what to toss. The best part? Coming up with fresh new ways to rework her wardrobe, then making a wish list of items needed to complete her look. Reward yourselves with a latte and stroll; sometimes the greatest inspiration comes from window-shopping and a meaningful heart-to-heart.

5

Calm, Cool, Collected Mom

Before yoga retreats were de rigueur and looking after ourselves became a hashtag (#selfcaresunday), mom knew the value of Me Time. ‘Take care of yourself to take care of others’ is her mantra—a lesson she has proudly passed down to her children. Honour her Zen-like ways with a special Mother’s Day itinerary focused on rest and relaxation.

Treat her to new yoga accessories and book a restorative yoga class or a silent meditation that’ll leave you both feeling refreshed. Or enjoy a lazy brunch, followed by a wander through a cool little neighbourhood you rarely get to explore. Our favourites have it all: wellness shops, juice bars, graffiti art and more. Mother’s Day also provides the perfect opportunity for experiencing something new together, be it a creative workshop (weaving or photography, for instance), a gallery exhibition or an Indigo Events author appearance and book signing.
