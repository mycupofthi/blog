Tell us about your relationship with your mother growing up.

“I am one of four children that were very close in age, and we all had a wonderful relationship with our mom. It was a relationship in a warm family, with a huge commitment from her to expose us to all of the things that she loved, including ballet, art, language, piano, dance and reading. At the same time, she was unintentionally blazing a trail. She was the original multitasker; a woman who could be a wife, a mother and have a career. And by virtue of how she did it, she created an example that said ‘It’s okay, you can do these things.’”

How have your mother’s teachings inspired you to become the person you are today?

“She was, and still is, a role model in several ways. She reflected, and lived, a life where a woman could imagine having a family, being a wife, and having the ability to follow your individual passions. That notion that you could have a multi-faceted life was important. She has always treated me with respect—even as a child. She believes in treating everyone with kindness and respect, no matter who they are or what they do.”

“My mother has always expressed that you can be feminine and bold at the same time.”

What values did you learn from your mother that you’ve passed on to your children and grandchildren?

“Exposure to experiences and activities, and encouraging independence and respect. She made family gatherings and family time together central to our lives. My parents were role models by sharing the value of marriage and family. I experienced all of these things and I believe they hugely influenced the parent and grandparent I am today.”

What advice would you give mothers raising children in today’s fast-paced, digital world?

“First and foremost as a role model, what you do, how you spend your time and how you are with others, that is, more or less, who your children will become. As much as everyday seems so busy, the time when our children are home with us is fleeting. Everything you invest now will pay in the future. Be conscious of the way in which you spend your time, as much as possible. Be present; be tuned in; be conscious.”

“My favourite Mother’s Day ever was having my mom, my children and my grandchildren all together.”

What is one of your favourite Mother’s Day memories?

“One year when I was a teenager, I gave her a special framed card, which included all of the things I had learned from her—she still has it. But my favourite Mother’s Day ever was having my mom, my children and my grandchildren all together. These days my mom, who is 98, doesn’t travel so my favourite way to spend time together is to have a short visit or call, and then, if we can find ourselves in the same city, being with all of my kids and grandkids. Time together is meaningful, especially on Mother’s Day.”
